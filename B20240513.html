Damage of Material via Electric Field (Photon)

Avalanche Breakdown of Electron: Avalanche Ionization

"No snowflake in an avalanche ever feels responsible."

— Voltaire

When discussing the avalanche of snow in nature, it is understood to be caused by the imbalance between the gravity from Earth and the electromagnetic force from all the snow. Similarly, from the perspective of an electron avalanche in materials, the imbalance is caused between the strong electric field from an external laser and the electromagnetic force binding the electrons within atoms.

How to quantify the occurrence of an electron becoming free from binding?

The answer lies in the energy required to overcome, commonly known as ionization energy, denoted as \( \epsilon_{\text{ion}} \) (ionization energy).

If the total energy absorbed by a group of electrons is \( E_{\text{total}} = NPdt \), where \( N \) is the number of electrons and \( P \) is the absorbed power for each electron by the external electric field, the change in number can be expressed as:

\[ dN = \frac{fE_{\text{total}}}{\epsilon_{\text{ion}}} = \frac{fNPdt}{\epsilon_{\text{ion}}} \]

Here, \( f \) represents the fraction of energy used for ionization, with the remaining \( (1-f) \) converted into heat.
