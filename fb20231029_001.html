【月亮的聯想】

當晚的信件中，

與Lela分享了:

(今)Today (人)people (不)doesn't (見)observe (古)ancient (時)time (月)moon,

(今)Today (月)moon (曾)has (經)once (照)illuminated (古)ancient (人)people.

_Bai Lee

(Bai Lee, the most renowned poet of the Tang Dynasty, is often compared to 'George Gordon Byron' in English literature.)

我實在不知道如何將古典文學中的美感，

與西方人分享，所謂感懷、長嘆...

#其實想想無問西東

#感懷這種東西還是小眾

＼

所幸，Lela與我分享了另一首1970的名曲:

「Moondance」

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6lFxGBB4UGU

使我聯想到早十幾年的另一首

「Fly me to the moon」

便開始認真思考，兩首曲子的差異。

Moondance的節奏更加輕快，情緒的鋪墊和上升也明顯，

整首歌曲充滿著自由的情感。

我才恍然大悟，歌如其名，人都已經在月亮上了，

早已拋開一切塵俗的煩擾(nostalgia)，跳舞去。

然而，Fly me to the moon，步調較和諧穩當，底層藏著深深的自由。

可從表面聽來，仍舊停留於登月的嚮往，

然而，登與不登，好似也沒那麼重要，

這過程中的『玩耍』，已經被完整地唱出來。

Fly me to the moon會更加吸引我駐足聆聽，

或許是因為此曲和『欲上青天攬明月』的情感更加扣合吧!

＼

我從來沒有當太空人、登月、火星殖民的夢想或嚮往

因為自從被科學啟蒙以來，

我深信那值得探究一輩子的事，

無窮盡的在生活的各處，等我們去挖掘。

「生活中不是沒有美，而是缺少發現美的眼睛。」＿羅丹

Morning Lela,

Thank you for sharing 'Moondance' with me. The relaxing vibe reminds me of Frank Sinatra, my favorite English-language singer. Before one can dance on the moon, they must first "fly to the moon".

I've been listening to 'Moondance' and 'Fly Me to the Moon' on repeat to discern the nuanced differences between the two.

In my intuition, 'Moondance' exudes a more direct sense of freedom. It may stem from the idea that Van Morrison is already on the moon, without any nostalgic ties to Earth. He seems to enjoy each moment, dancing freely, which is truly cool!

On the other hand, 'Fly Me to the Moon' gives me a sense of aspiration to reach the moon, but at a leisurely, unhurried pace. Everything feels as if it can be aspired to, pursued, and played with.

Frank Sinatra typically exudes a joyful demeanor and a mindset filled with curiosity.
